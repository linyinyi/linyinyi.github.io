## Course overview

Development questions in China increasingly demand evidence at a scale and speed that traditional statistics cannot provide. This course teaches postgraduate students to answer those questions with geospatial big data: satellite observations, open geospatial datasets, and administrative data, analyzed with GIS workflows and cloud computing. Students move from data acquisition through spatial analysis to communicating results — building a complete, reproducible analysis of a development question they choose.

## Main topics

- Geospatial big data: sources, structures, and limitations
- Remote sensing fundamentals and satellite data products
- GIS workflows for development analysis
- Cloud-based analysis with Google Earth Engine
- Spatial analysis and spatial statistics
- Urban and regional development applications — urbanization, environment, infrastructure
- Reproducible research practice

## Methods and software

The course works hands-on with GIS software, Google Earth Engine, and scripted analysis workflows, emphasizing open data and reproducibility over any single tool.

## Teaching philosophy

I teach methods through questions, not the other way around. Every technique in the course is introduced by a real development problem it can help answer, and students spend most of their time applying methods to data rather than watching demonstrations. The goal is that students leave able to scope a spatial question, find and evaluate the data to answer it, and defend their analytical choices.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of the course, students can:

- locate, evaluate, and preprocess geospatial datasets relevant to a development question;
- design and execute a GIS and remote-sensing analysis workflow, including at cloud scale;
- apply appropriate spatial-analysis methods and interpret their outputs critically;
- present spatial evidence clearly, with honest treatment of uncertainty and data limitations.
