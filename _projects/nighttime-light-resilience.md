**Research question.** Can monthly nighttime-light observations measure community resilience directly — capturing how places dim after climate and social shocks and how quickly they brighten again?

**Scale.** Urban and regional scale, with multi-year monthly time series.

**Data.** Monthly satellite nighttime-light composites paired with records of climate extremes and social disruptions.

**Methods.** Time-series analysis of disturbance and recovery in nighttime-light intensity, event alignment against documented shocks, and comparison of recovery trajectories across cities and regions.

**Why nighttime lights.** Most resilience indicators are built from slow-moving census or survey data. Nighttime lights are observed globally, monthly, and independently of reporting capacity — which makes them one of the few longitudinal signals able to show recovery as it happens, including in places where administrative data are weakest.
