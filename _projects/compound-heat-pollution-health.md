**Research question.** How much mortality risk do cities underestimate when urban heat islands and air-pollution islands are assessed separately rather than as a compound exposure?

**Scale.** Cross-city analysis of Global North cities.

**Data.** Multisource satellite-derived environmental exposure — land surface and air temperature, fine particulate air pollution — combined with city-level mortality and population data.

**Methods.** Satellite exposure mapping, delineation of compound urban heat and air-pollution islands, structural equation modelling, and multiscale spatial analysis linking compound exposure to mortality and its social gradients.

**Key contribution.** The study shows that mortality risks from compound urban heat and air-pollution islands are underestimated when the two hazards are treated independently, and it maps how the compound burden — and the environmental health inequality it creates — varies across and within cities.

**Team.** Led by Yinyi Lin with collaborators at The University of Hong Kong and partner institutions (see the author list of the paper).
