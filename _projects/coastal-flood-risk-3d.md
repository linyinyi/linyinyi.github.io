**Research question.** Who is exposed to coastal flooding when risk is assessed in three dimensions — flood depth, building height, and the people inside — rather than as a flat inundation footprint?

**Scale.** Building-scale analysis in Hong Kong, with methods designed to transfer to coastal cities globally.

**Data.** High-resolution flood-depth mapping, three-dimensional building data, and fine-grained demographic information on older adults and other vulnerable groups.

**Methods.** Coupling inundation modelling with building-scale exposure assessment, and stratifying exposure by age and vulnerability to identify where adaptation needs concentrate.

**Why it matters.** Two districts with the same flooded area can face very different risks if one houses ground-floor elderly communities and the other high-rise offices. Resolving exposure to the building level turns flood maps into evidence that adaptation planning — evacuation, retrofitting, service placement — can act on.
