I teach at the intersection of geospatial data science and development studies. My courses emphasize working with real data at scale — satellite observations, administrative statistics, and open geospatial datasets — and building reproducible analysis workflows that students can carry into research and professional practice.

## GEOG7142 · Big Data and GIS for China Development Studies

**The University of Hong Kong · Spring 2023 and Spring 2024**

A postgraduate course teaching students to answer development questions in China with geospatial big data: satellite observations, open geospatial datasets, and administrative data, analyzed with GIS workflows, Google Earth Engine, and spatial analysis. Students move from data acquisition through spatial analysis to communicating results — building a complete, reproducible analysis of a development question they choose.

## Mentoring and supervision

I am glad to talk with students interested in GeoAI, remote sensing, urban environmental health, or climate resilience — whether about research directions, methods, or graduate study.
